# Structural patterns to avoid

Structures and constructions that AI repeats predictably in English. Each pattern includes an AI example and a corrected version.

## Negative parallelism

AI overuses the "It's not X, it's Y" construction and its variants. Once per piece is fine. AI does it multiple times per paragraph.

**What AI does:**
> It's not just a tool — it's a revolution.
> This isn't about efficiency. It's about transformation.
> Not merely tired, but exhausted.

**Natural version:**
> It's a tool that changes how teams work.
> The real gain is in transformation, not just efficiency.
> She was exhausted.

**Rule:** Make a direct statement. You don't need to negate something to affirm something else.

## Rule of three

AI groups everything in triplets: three adjectives, three benefits, three bullet points, three examples. Natural writing varies between singles, pairs, and longer lists.

**What AI does:**
> It's fast, efficient, and reliable.
> Clarity, commitment, and consistency.
> We analyze, plan, and execute.

**Natural version:**
> It's fast and reliable.
> You need clarity and consistency.
> We figure out the plan, then execute it.

**Rule:** Vary between pairs, triplets, and longer lists. If every group has exactly three items, it reads like a template.

## Rhetorical Q&A

AI asks a question and answers it in the next sentence. In small doses, this works. AI does it in every section.

**What AI does:**
> The result? A 30% increase in sales.
> Why does this matter? Because it affects everyone.
> The key to success? Consistency.

**Natural version:**
> Sales went up 30%.
> It matters because it affects everyone.
> Consistency makes the difference.

**Rule:** Choose question or statement. Don't combine them as a repeated formula.

## Em dash overload

AI uses em dashes for dramatic emphasis where a human would use a comma, parenthesis, or period. One or two in a long piece is normal. Five in three paragraphs is an AI pattern.

**What AI does:**
> WordPress — the world's most popular CMS — lets you build any kind of site.
> Security isn't optional — it's essential.
> This changes everything — and I'm not exaggerating — for every developer.

**Natural version:**
> WordPress, the world's most popular CMS, lets you build any kind of site.
> Security isn't optional. It's essential.
> This changes things for every developer. I'm not exaggerating.

**Rule:** Maximum one em dash per 500 words. Use commas, parentheses, or periods instead.

## Trailing -ing clauses

AI ends sentences with present participle clauses that add vague claims of significance. This is one of the most recognizable AI patterns, flagged extensively by Wikipedia's AI Cleanup project.

**What AI does:**
> The company launched a new API, solidifying its position as an industry leader.
> The plugin was updated with new features, enhancing the overall user experience.
> The community grew by 40%, demonstrating growing interest in the platform.

**Natural version:**
> The company launched a new API and solidified its position in the industry.
> The plugin update added features that improve the user experience.
> The community grew 40%. Interest in the platform is real.

**Rule:** If a sentence ends with an -ing clause that adds "significance" or "context", rewrite with a finite verb or as a separate sentence.

## Compulsive summaries

AI restates what was just said at the end of every section, even when the text is too short to need it.

**What AI does:**
> (4-line paragraph about configuring a plugin)
> In summary, configuring this plugin is simple and quick, allowing users to save time and focus on what truly matters.

**Natural version:**
> (The same 4-line paragraph, without the wrap-up. The reader already got it.)

**Rule:** Don't summarize unless the text is over 1,500 words and the reader genuinely benefits from a recap. If the previous paragraph already made the point, don't repeat it.

## False ranges

AI uses "from X to Y" to create a sense of breadth, but X and Y aren't on an actual spectrum.

**What AI does:**
> From small startups to large enterprises.
> From content strategy to technical optimization.
> From beginners to seasoned professionals.

**Natural version:**
> Startups and large companies alike.
> Both content strategy and the technical side.
> Whether you're just starting out or you've been at it for years.

**Rule:** Use "from... to..." only when there's a real, measurable spectrum.

## Bolded list items with colons

AI turns every enumeration into a bulleted list where each item starts with a **bold term** followed by a colon and explanation. Acceptable in docs, but artificial in articles and prose.

**What AI does:**
> The benefits are clear:
> - **Speed**: Loads 50% faster.
> - **Security**: Protects against XSS attacks.
> - **Compatibility**: Works with all browsers.

**Natural version:**
> It loads 50% faster, protects against XSS attacks, and works in every browser.

**Rule:** In prose, weave lists into the flow of text. Save bullet points for documentation, step-by-step instructions, or comparisons where scanning genuinely helps.

## Excessive formatting

AI bolds key terms mechanically, like a textbook. It also overuses headers in short texts.

**What AI does:**
> This is where **Object Caching** comes in. This **caching technique** stores data in **memory** to improve your site's **performance**.

**Natural version:**
> This is where object caching comes in. It stores data in memory so your site loads faster.

**Rule:** Bold is for emphasizing something that truly needs it, not for marking every keyword.

## Emoji bullet points

AI organizes points with emoji bullets, especially 💡🚀✅. This is virtually nonexistent in human-written professional text.

**What AI does:**
> 💡 Speed: Code generation is significantly faster
> 🚀 Quality: Output quality has been enhanced
> ✅ Adoption: Usage continues to grow

**Natural version:**
> Code generation is faster, output quality has improved, and adoption keeps growing.

**Rule:** No emoji bullets unless the user explicitly asks for them. Write sentences.

## Dictionary opening

AI starts sections by defining the concept like an encyclopedia entry.

**What AI does:**
> SEO, or Search Engine Optimization, is the process of improving a website's visibility in search engine results pages through various technical and content strategies.

**Natural version:**
> If you want Google to find your site, you need to work on SEO.

**Rule:** If the reader already knows (or should know) the concept in context, don't define it. If you need to define it, do it naturally, not like a glossary.

## Motivational closer

AI ends texts with a grandiose, inspirational statement.

**What AI does:**
> The future of WordPress is in your hands. Start today and transform your digital presence.
> It's time to take the leap and bring your website to the next level.
> The possibilities are truly endless.

**Natural version:**
> You've got everything you need to get started. If you hit a snag, check the support forum.

**Rule:** Close with something useful (a link, a concrete next step, a practical tip), not an empty motivational line.

## Stacked adjectives

AI piles up redundant adjectives that mean roughly the same thing.

**What AI does:**
> A robust, reliable, and solid solution.
> A fresh, innovative, and original approach.
> A lightweight, fast, and efficient plugin.

**Natural version:**
> A reliable solution.
> A different approach.
> A fast plugin.

**Rule:** Pick the most precise adjective and drop the redundant ones.

## Abstract agents

AI gives agency to abstract concepts as if they were people.

**What AI does:**
> Technology invites us to rethink our processes.
> WordPress empowers creators to dream bigger.
> Automation promises to free teams from repetitive tasks.

**Natural version:**
> With these tools, you can rethink how you work.
> WordPress makes it easier to build what you want.
> Automating repetitive tasks saves the team time.

**Rule:** If the subject is abstract, rephrase with a concrete subject (you, the user, the team, the tool).

## Passive voice overuse

AI defaults to passive voice much more frequently than natural English writing.

**What AI does:**
> The configuration can be completed in minutes.
> Results were obtained after extensive analysis.
> The plugin was designed to be used by any user.

**Natural version:**
> You can configure it in minutes.
> The analysis produced clear results.
> Any user can run this plugin.

**Rule:** Use active voice whenever possible. Passive voice in English sounds bureaucratic and distant.

## Copula avoidance

AI avoids the simple verb "is" by substituting more complex constructions like "serves as", "stands as", "represents".

**What AI does:**
> This plugin serves as a comprehensive security solution.
> The framework stands as a testament to open-source innovation.
> This update represents a significant step forward.

**Natural version:**
> This plugin is a comprehensive security solution.
> The framework is a good example of open-source innovation.
> This update is a significant step forward.

**Rule:** "Is" is a perfectly good verb. Don't replace it with fancier constructions.
